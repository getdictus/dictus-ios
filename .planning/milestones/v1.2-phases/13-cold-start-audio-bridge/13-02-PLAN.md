<objective>
SwipeBackOverlayView: the full-screen branded overlay that guides users to swipe back to the keyboard after cold start.

Purpose: When iOS has killed the app and the keyboard opens it, the user should NOT see the full app UI (tabs, HomeView). Instead, they see a clean, branded screen with an animated hand gesture showing how to swipe back. This is the key UX differentiator from the current "app just opens normally" experience.

Output: SwipeBackOverlayView with SwiftUI-animated swipe gesture, brand gradient, bilingual text. MainTabView updated to render it instead of the placeholder from Plan 01.

   - Full-screen ZStack with brand gradient background: `LinearGradient(colors: [Color(hex: "0D2040"), Color(hex: "071020")], startPoint: .topLeading, endPoint: .bottomTrailing).ignoresSafeArea()`
   - NOTE: Check if `Color(hex:)` extension exists in the project. If not, use `Color(red:green:blue:)` with converted values:
     - #0D2040 = Color(red: 0.051, green: 0.125, blue: 0.251)
     - #071020 = Color(red: 0.027, green: 0.063, blue: 0.125)
   - VStack centered vertically containing:
     a. Spacer
     b. SwipeAnimationView (internal subview, see below) -- frame ~200x300
     c. Primary text: FR "Glisse pour revenir au clavier" / EN "Swipe back to the keyboard" -- `.font(.title2.weight(.semibold))`, `.foregroundColor(.white)`
     d. Secondary text: FR "Glisse vers la droite en bas de l'ecran" / EN "Swipe right on the bottom of your iPhone" -- `.font(.subheadline)`, `.foregroundColor(.white.opacity(0.6))`
     e. Spacer

   NOTE: French text uses correct accents: "ecran" should be "l'ecran" (the accent on e may not render in code, use the proper unicode character e with accent grave: \u{00E9} for e-accent-aigu in "Glisse" is not needed, but "ecran" needs "\u{00E9}cran" -> "l'\u{00E9}cran"). Actually the CONTEXT.md specifies the exact strings -- use those verbatim:
   - FR primary: "Glisse pour revenir au clavier"
   - FR secondary: "Glisse vers la droite en bas de l'ecran" (note: CONTEXT.md has this without accent, keep as specified by user)
   - EN primary: "Swipe back to the keyboard"
   - EN secondary: "Swipe right on the bottom of your iPhone"

2. **SwipeAnimationView** -- internal private struct:
   Design inspired by SuperWhisper's overlay (per CONTEXT.md). Create a simplified iPhone outline with an animated hand/thumb performing a right swipe on the home indicator area.

   Implementation approach (SwiftUI only, no Lottie per user decision):
   - iPhone outline: RoundedRectangle with thin white stroke (opacity 0.3), aspect ratio ~9:19.5 (iPhone proportions), corner radius ~30
   - Home indicator bar: Small RoundedRectangle at bottom center (width ~80, height ~5, white opacity 0.4)
   - Animated thumb/circle: A circle (diameter ~30) with accent color (#3D7EFF) that starts from center-bottom and slides right repeatedly
   - Animation: Use `.offset(x:)` with a repeating animation. The circle starts at x: -30 and moves to x: +50, with `.animation(.easeInOut(duration: 1.5).repeatForever(autoreverses: false), value: isAnimating)` triggered by `onAppear { isAnimating = true }`
   - Add an arrow trail: small chevron or opacity fade behind the moving circle to indicate swipe direction

   This should feel clean and instructional, not flashy. The key visual: "swipe right from the bottom of the screen."

3. **Preview provider** with both FR and EN variants.

WHY no Lottie: User explicitly chose SwiftUI animation only (locked decision from CONTEXT.md).
